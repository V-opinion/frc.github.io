Why Transition from Church Elections to Multi-Religion Election Systems?

In countries such as Sweden, the Church of Sweden has held democratic church elections since 2000. These elections are open, inclusive, and offer multilingual, postal, and accessible voting options.

Globally, most countries do not have similar mechanisms. A multi-religion election system would allow different faith communities to democratically select leadership, ensuring that democracy permeates every religious institutionwhether in the USA, UK, China, India, Iran, or Israel.
